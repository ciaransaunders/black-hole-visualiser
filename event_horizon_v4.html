About
Event Horizon v4 is a real-time relativistic black hole visualizer rendered entirely in a fragment shader. It approximates the Kerr metric for spinning black holes and includes Doppler beaming, frame-dragging, photon-ring sub-images, and the full set of EHT-style polarimetric and redshift overlays.
Recent Observations
2019 — EHT first image of M87*.
2021 — M87* polarimetric image revealed ordered magnetic field.
2022 — EHT image of Sgr A* released.
2024 — Sgr A* polarimetric image confirmed similar field structure; M87* time-variability image published.
2025 — LIGO-Virgo-KAGRA announce GW231123, the most massive binary BH merger yet observed (~225 M☉).
Notes
Mass slider is in geometric units (gravitational radii relative to scene scale). Each preset overrides this with its real physical mass for the displayed quantities. The Kerr ISCO and photon sphere formulas use the standard a/M dimensionless spin. Hawking temperature accounts for spin: T → 0 at extremal Kerr.

Physics presets

Each preset configures the simulator with parameters drawn from observation: M87* (a/M≈0.9, i≈17°, ~6.5×10⁹ M☉), Sgr A* (a/M≈0.5, ~4.3×10⁶ M☉), Cygnus X-1 (a/M≥0.998, ~21 M☉), OJ 287 (binary, ~18×10⁹ M☉ primary), TON 618 (~6.6×10¹⁰ M☉), GW190521 (~142 M☉ remnant), GW231123 (~225 M☉ remnant — most massive BBH yet), Gargantua (Interstellar: a/M≈0.998, i≈85°).

Performance

If the simulator runs slowly, switch to Performance quality in the System tab, lower the resolution scale, or switch off the Milky Way / lensing magnification (these are the heaviest modules). The render automatically pauses when the tab is hidden.
